Use VGI to Identify and Analyze Top Tourist Attractions

The Problems

In the past, most information of tourist attractions was provided by authoritative organizations. However, the process of collection is time- and labor- consuming, and the data may be incomplete. With the development of GPS technology and the Internet, the public can record and share their locations on the website. This kind of citizen-generated geographic information is called Volunteered Geographic Information (VGI).

Objectives

This research aimed to evaluate the effectiveness of VGI in identification and analysis of the top tourist attractions. It took Toronto as an example, and presented an approach to collect information on geotagged photos from Flickr. The top tourist attractions and their seasonal variations were identified and analyzed by mapping the density and hot spots of these photos.
